# NEXUS RAID

**Concept:** Top-down arena wave-shooter where your chosen Core (Shield/Blade/Flux) changes the physics rules of combat — same enemies, three fundamentally different games.
**Player fantasy:** I am my toolkit — my Core shapes how I think, not just what I press.

## Core Loop
Select Core at start screen → arena spawns → waves of drones/tanks attack → survive using your Core's unique physics contract → defeat Boss at wave 5 → earn a Core Upgrade (2-option pick, no RNG filler) → repeat in harder arena.

## Key Mechanics
- **Shield Core** — zero offense; angle your deflector to redirect enemy projectiles back at them
- **Blade Core** — charge-and-lunge chain; hit resets cooldown, miss = wall stun; high skill ceiling
- **Flux Core** — charge timing puzzle; short burst vs. full-charge AoE that risks self-damage

## What's Built
Thesis tested: Sonic Rumble's 10M signups / $1.6M revenue failure (cosmetic identity → no retention) inverted. All three Cores are mechanically legible from the first enemy encounter — Shield players think in angles, Blade players think in chains, Flux players think in positioning. Divergent skill acquisition starts before minute one. Prototype confirms mechanical identity creates different competencies per player, not just different aesthetics.
