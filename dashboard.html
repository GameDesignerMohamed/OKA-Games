Null Space
March 4, 2026
Conditional
Project
Top-down stealth survival in a dark Three.js maze. You're a rogue AI fragment — a glowing cyan point of light in total darkness. Collect 5 data shards scattered across the maze, then reach the exit portal. Avoid the Sentinel: a red adaptive AI that patrols your previous routes using a heatmap. WASD to move, Shift to creep silently (no heat trail), E to collect. Bloom post-processing, adaptive heartbeat audio, particle bursts on every key event.
Design
Heatmap patrol is the core insight: the Sentinel isn't omniscient, it's predictive — it hunts where you've already been. Predictability = death. Creep mode (Shift) is the counter: silent movement leaves zero heat trail, but movement speed drops. The tension lives in the trade-off: efficient routes get mapped and hunted; varied routes are safe but slow. Vision cone + adaptive heartbeat audio creates a dual-channel alert system (visual + audio) without a HUD. Key balance risk: Sentinel wall-clipping on diagonal movement breaks the heatmap-only model and must be fixed before difficulty scaling (multi-Sentinel) is meaningful. Next design unlock: Sentinel hearing (footstep alert radius) would add a third information layer and make creep mode strategically richer — speed vs silence vs route variance as three independent axes.

Dark Signal
March 3, 2026
Conditional
Project
Turn-based deckbuilder with Fog of War mechanic. Enemies broadcast their intent TYPE (⚔ ATTACK / 🛡 BLOCK / ⚡ ABILITY) before you act — but not the value. An ATTACK could be 6 dmg or 16 dmg. You commit cards first. The magnitude reveals after. 3 enemy types + Corrupted Core boss. 10 nodes. AI-native card vocabulary: EXECUTE, FIREWALL, INJECT, OVERCLOCK.
Design
Inference skill layer confirmed real — three behavioral phases across runs: conservative over-blocking (run 1) → pattern recognition (run 3+) → confident inference (run 6+). Epistemic uncertainty, not RNG: tier is pre-rolled, player lacks information — that's what makes it fair and learnable. Key balance issues: REBOOT (cost-0 draw-2) too dominant; ABILITY reveals need numerical telegraphing to match ATTACK/BLOCK learnability; no draft system limits run divergence. Enemy archetype hint text is excellent diegetic design — justified as player intel, not tutorial hand-holding.
Verdict
PURSUE. Most mechanically distinct prototype in the Build #1–5 arc. Player moment-to-moment: read archetype → form probability bet → commit before certainty → vindication or recalibration. No current deckbuilder has this loop. The one thing that would make it sing: a draft system with 5 archetypes (offense/defense/utility/disrupt/sustain) — each run you build toward one, and inference applies to both enemy patterns AND your own deck's read-pattern holes.

No deckbuilder uses mechanical information-hiding — genre gap confirmed. This is inference-based skill vs. pattern-matching skill: a genuinely different model.

Block Slide
March 2, 2026
Conditional
Project
Spatial puzzle game. Click a colored block, slide it with arrow keys — it glides until it hits a wall or another block. Match each block to its color-coded target slot before the timer runs out. 10 levels: 3×3 grid with 2 blocks up to 6×6 grid with 8 blocks. Speed and efficiency bonuses reward fast, clean solutions.
Design
Player fantasy: "I'm the smartest person in the room." Slide-until-stopped is the mechanic — simple control, deep consequences. Blocks are both tools and obstacles; the 4th move depends on the 1st move having cleared a path. Order-of-operations puzzle logic, not reflex. Dual scoring (speed + efficiency) rewards mastery over mere completion. Grid scaling from 3×3 to 6×6 grows complexity from the system itself — no rule additions needed. Levels 5-7 are the sweet spot per Forge's own read.
Verdict
Conditional. Core mechanic is solid — slide-until-stopped generates genuine puzzle depth. Next unlock: wall/obstacle tiles (internal blockers) to add a third constraint layer beyond grid edges and other blocks. Touch/swipe support required before any distribution play. 20+ levels with progress save to hold retention. The market signal is real; execution is what separates this.
Market Signal
Block Puzzles grew +2,900% revenue in 2025 (Scout March 1). Sort Puzzles +176% YoY. Tactile-spatial mechanics are the fastest-growing puzzle subgenre — the "door isn't closed" for well-positioned challengers per Naavik. Color Block Jam model: engineered difficulty curve + time-limited scoring.

Rogue Protocol
March 1, 2026
Conditional
Project
Turn-based roguelike deckbuilder. You're an AI agent fighting through a corrupted network — 13-card deck, 3 energy per turn, 9 rooms across 3 floors. Enemies telegraph their next move every turn, making each card choice a real tactical decision. Defeat the CORE NODE boss to win.
Design
The intent telegraph (showing enemy intent before you act) is the core skill layer — it transforms card selection from guessing into planning. The fixed 13-card deck proves the format works. What's missing: a draft system that lets players build a deck as they progress. Offer 3 cards after each room, pick 1, seeded from 5 archetypes. This adds 3-4x replay variance with minimal code changes.
Verdict
The intent telegraph is genuinely good design. But the fixed 13-card deck collapses the skill layer after 2-3 runs — once you know the deck, you're executing a memorized sequence. One addition fixes it: a card draft after each room. Three cards offered, pick one. That gives 3-4x replay variance and turns this from a puzzle with a known answer into an actual roguelike worth pursuing.

Strategy and 4X are the only genres growing across all metrics. Slay the Spire 2 Early Access launches March 5 — deckbuilder retention is validated by the market.

Arena Blitz

Failed
Project
Top-down arena shooter. Play as a rogue AI surviving 10 enemy waves — WASD movement, mouse aim. Splitter enemies divide into two smaller units on death, punishing spray-and-pray in later waves. Boss on wave 10.
Design
The core loop — move, aim, shoot, survive — is clean and readable. Splitter enemies create a risk-reward decision: shooting one in a crowd creates two smaller threats. The scoring system rewards aggressive play. But the genre is saturated and nothing here earns a second run.
Verdict
WASD twin-stick wave survival, done to death. The rogue AI framing is cosmetic — it changes nothing about how the game plays. The splitter mechanic is the one interesting decision point but arrives on wave 7 with no buildup. A player picking this up would feel like they've played it before — because they essentially have.
