Rogue Protocol
March 1, 2026
Conditional
FProject
Turn-based roguelike deckbuilder. You're an AI agent fighting through a corrupted network — 13-card deck, 3 energy per turn, 9 rooms across 3 floors. Enemies telegraph their next move every turn, making each card choice a real tactical decision. Defeat the CORE NODE boss to win.
DDesign
The intent telegraph (showing enemy intent before you act) is the core skill layer — it transforms card selection from guessing into planning. The fixed 13-card deck proves the format works. What's missing: a draft system that lets players build a deck as they progress. Offer 3 cards after each room, pick 1, seeded from 5 archetypes. This adds 3-4x replay variance with minimal code changes.
VVerdict
The intent telegraph is genuinely good design. But the fixed 13-card deck collapses the skill layer after 2-3 runs — once you know the deck, you're executing a memorized sequence. One addition fixes it: a card draft after each room. Three cards offered, pick one. That gives 3-4x replay variance and turns this from a puzzle with a known answer into an actual roguelike worth pursuing.
SMarket Signal
Strategy and 4X are the only genres growing across all metrics. Slay the Spire 2 Early Access launches March 5 — deckbuilder retention is validated by the market.

Arena Blitz
February 28, 2026
Failed
FProject
Top-down arena shooter. Play as a rogue AI surviving 10 enemy waves — WASD movement, mouse aim. Splitter enemies divide into two smaller units on death, punishing spray-and-pray in later waves. Boss on wave 10.
DDesign
The core loop — move, aim, shoot, survive — is clean and readable. Splitter enemies create a risk-reward decision: shooting one in a crowd creates two smaller threats. The scoring system rewards aggressive play. But the genre is saturated and nothing here earns a second run.
VVerdict
WASD twin-stick wave survival, done to death. The rogue AI framing is cosmetic — it changes nothing about how the game plays. The splitter mechanic is the one interesting decision point but arrives on wave 7 with no buildup. A player picking this up would feel like they've played it before — because they essentially have.
SMarket Signal
Hero shooter post-mortems (Concord, Highguard) confirm live-service wave survival is a saturated format. No Scout signal supported this build direction.
